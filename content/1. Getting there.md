## Border duties

### Pre-requisites

1. Bikes need to be **road registered** and **licenced beyond** the Expedition end date.
2. **Original eNatis certificate** is essential to travel with owner / driver.
3. **Clearance certificate** from police station – x **3 copies**. Every Driver of every motorcycle must **list every attachment and tool on the bike** even extras such as lights. 
4. **Don't leave loose things** - Tools, GPS etc. on the bikes - bring them with you or place them in a separate bag for transporting. 
5. Get a **clearance certificate** from the police that states its not stolen and it belongs to you.
6. Prepare an **authorization letter** confirming a driver can transport it to Namibia for you.
	1. *Can the above clearance / letters all be done as one ??*
7. It is up to each **bike owner to insure their own bikes** and **notify their insurer** of the trip details.
8. Mike to confirm bike trailer is insured etc.
9. Copies of key documents (passports, ID etc.) to be made.
10. Medical Aid to be notified of the trip.
11. **Bikes and documents delivered** to TBA on the TBA for loading; with 4 x labelled Tie-down straps (non Chinese - preferably narrow ratchet), Shock protector.

### Border crossing requirements / details
 
1. **3rd party insurance** will be need to be purchased when crossing into Namibia
2. The **vehicle transporting** the bikes will also need to take out 3rd party insurance for the transporting vehicle & trailer. 
3. All insurance referred to above are **paid on the Namibia side of the border** (Not something that is an additional insurance cover from you SA insurance company)
4. The people taking the bikes across the border is likely to spend some time at the border dealing with with this schlepp. The things going over the border must com back - they want to check everything that was on the bike is still on and hasn’t been sold.
